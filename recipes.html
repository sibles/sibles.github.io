Cake tatin aka apple upside-down cake

Note:This is not an accurate image of the cake, it was chosen so it can be displayed on khan accadamy.

  • Time: 30 minutes - 1 hour
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Granny Smith apples, peeled, quartered, core removed 2
Cator Sugar 1 cup
butter 100g (25g chopped, 75g melted)
salt 1/4 tsp
self-raising flour 1 cup
ground cinnamon 2 tsp
eggs 2
milk 1/2 cup
vanilla extract 1 tsp
vanilla ice-cream to serve

Steps:

  1. Heat your oven to 180C.
  2. Cut each apple quarter into quarters again to create 16 slices for each apple.
  3. Melt ¼ cup sugar in a 26cm ovenproof frying pan over medium-low heat.
  4. Swirl the pan so the sugar covers the base and cook until golden, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add 25g chopped butter and half the salt, swirling the pan until the butter melts and combines with the sugar.
  6. Add the apples and cook for 2 minutes each side, then turn off the heat.
  7. Using tongs, arrange the apple pieces into a radial pattern in the pan. Set aside.
  8. Combine remaining ¾ cup sugar, flour, cinnamon and remaining salt in a large bowl.
  9. Whisk the eggs, milk, vanilla extract and 75g melted butter in a separate bowl.
  10. Make a well in the centre of the flour mix and pour in the milk mixture. Whisk until just combined.
  11. Pour the batter into the pan, then bake in the oven for 15 minutes or until golden and cooked through when tested with a skewer.
  12. Remove and allow to cool for 10 minutes.
  13. Turn the cake out upside-down and serve with vanilla ice-cream.

Spicy tuna melt nachos

Note:This is not an accurate image of the nachos, it was chosen so it can be displayed on khan accadamy.

  • Time: 0:15 Prep, 0:30 Cook
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
extra virgin olive oil 1 tablespoon
brown onion, chopped 1
garlic clove, crushed 1
ground cumin 2 teaspoons
ground coriander 1 teaspoon
smoked paprika 1 teaspoon
chilli powder 1/4 teaspoon
black beans, drained, rinsed 400g can
lime juice 1 tablespoon
chopped fresh coriander leaves 2 tablespoons
Sirena Tuna in Oil Italian Style, drained, flaked 2 x 185g cans
corn chips 200g packet
grated tasty cheese 1 1/2 cups
tomatoes, seeded, finely chopped 2
avocado, finely chopped 1
plain Greek-style yoghurt 1/3 cup
fresh jalapeño chillies, sliced 2
Fresh coriander sprigs to serve
Lime wedges to serve

Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Cook onion, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes or until softened. Add garlic, cumin, ground coriander, paprika and chilli powder. Cook, stirring, for 1 minute or until fragrant. Add beans and 1/3 cup water. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes or until mixture thickens and liquid has evaporated. Remove from heat. Stir in lime juice and chopped coriander. Toss through tuna.
  2. Preheat oven to 180C/160C fan-forced.
  3. Arrange half the corn chips in 4 heatproof serving dishes. Sprinkle with 1/2 cup cheese. Top with remaining corn chips, tuna mixture and sprinkle with remaining cheese. Bake for 15 minutes or until cheese has melted and tuna is heated through.
  4. Sprinkle nachos with tomato and avocado. Dollop with yoghurt and top with jalapeño and coriander sprigs. Serve with lime wedges.

Chocolate cake

Note:This is not an accurate image of the cake, it was chosen so it can be displayed on khan accadamy.

  • Time: 0:40 Prep, 1:10 Cook
  • Servings: 10
Ingredients Quantity
good-quality dark chocolate, roughly chopped 200g
unsalted butter, softened 250g
caster sugar 1 1/2 cups (330g)
vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
eggs 4
plain flour 2 cups (300g)
baking powder 1 teaspoon
bicarbonate of soda 1/2 teaspoon
almond meal 1/2 cup
NESTLÉ BAKERS' CHOICE Cocoa, sifted 1/3 cup

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C. Grease and line the base and sides of a 23cm springform cake pan.
  2. Place chocolate and 60g of the butter in a heatproof bowl over a pan of gently simmering water (don't let the bowl touch the water). Stir occasionally until melted, then set aside to cool.
  3. Place the sugar and remaining butter in an electric mixer and beat until pale and fluffy, then beat in the vanilla extract. Add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition, until just combined. Beat in the cooled chocolate mixture.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder and bicarbonate, then add the almond meal. In a third bowl, combine the cocoa powder and 2/3 cup (160ml) of boiling water. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the chocolate mixture, alternating with the cocoa mixture, until all the ingredients are combined.
  5. Pour mixture into prepared pan and bake in the oven for 1 hour or until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean. Remove from the oven, allow to cool slightly, then remove the collar of the pan and cool completely.
  6. If you wish to fill the cake, carefully cut it into 3 layers horizontally. Place the bottom layer on a serving plate, then use a spatula to spread with half the whipped chocolate filling (see related recipe). Place the middle layer on top, and spread with remaining filling. Place the top slice onto the cake and carefully pour over the glaze (see related recipe), letting it drip down the sides. Allow half an hour for the glaze to set before cutting.
